## Why this page exists

Every team has inside jokes. They're a real thing — they signal belonging, they make hard days easier, and they're the stuff a new hire is most likely to feel locked out of. We're putting ours in writing so you don't have to spend three months figuring out why someone keeps saying `<inside joke>` in the team channel.

If you read this and don't laugh, that's fine. You will after you've been here a month. If you read this and **do** laugh — congratulations, you're already one of us.

## Recurring bits

### "13 seconds for WSL to fail"

From the original onboarding deck. Refers to the average time between starting backend setup on Windows and discovering some bit of WSL didn't initialize correctly. Has since broadened to mean "the gap between confidence and reality".

Modern usage: "I'm 13 seconds away from realizing why this isn't working."

### "Paciência" as a requirement

The old onboarding deck listed "Patience" alongside Node and Docker as an environment requirement. It stayed because it's accurate. Now it's a load-bearing tradition — every environment setup page mentions it.

### "If we made mistakes, it was to check code review"

The quote from the old deck, now codified in [How we work](/getting-started/welcome/culture). Trotted out any time someone catches a bug a previous PR introduced.

## Rituals

### Deploy announcements

After every prod deploy, the deployer posts a message in Gmail Chat. The format is enforced by the `pr-creator` agent (see [pr-creator](/ai-workflows/internal-developer-workflows/agents/pr-creator#deploy-announcement-optional)):

```
Hey team, I just deployed the following to production:

- <Task name 1> — https://app.clickup.com/t/<task_code_1>
- <Task name 2> — https://app.clickup.com/t/<task_code_2>

Please give me a shout if anything looks off after the rollout. Thanks!
```

Em dashes, plain text, one bullet per ClickUp task. We're particular about this.

### Branch naming

`<change_type>/<task_code>`. `feat/`, `fix/`, `refactor/`. Anything else gets gently roasted in code review.

### "Sfink-flavored bug investigations"

When a customer report leads to a multi-step DB → Datadog → CloudWatch dive that ends with "actually the customer is calling the wrong flow", that's a Sfink-flavored investigation. The full methodology lives in [Flux error investigation](/ai-workflows/internal-developer-workflows/playbooks/flux-error-investigation). Named after Guilherme's nickname not because he's the only one who does them, but because he wrote the playbook.

### Cadence

The team's recurring meetings — block these on your calendar from day one:

| When | What |
| --- | --- |
| Every weekday at 10:30 BRT | Daily standup — short check-in, what you're doing, what's blocking you |
| Monday, Wednesday, Friday at 13:30 BRT | Customer meeting — sync with the client; agenda usually shared the day before |
| Every other Tuesday afternoon | Retro — what went well, what didn't, what we'll try differently |

Time zone is São Paulo (BRT, UTC−3). Most of the team is in Brazil; if you're working from elsewhere, do the math once and add the times to your calendar in your local zone.

## Tone in chat

A few patterns you'll notice:

- **Lowercase chat by default.** Capital letters mean someone is emphasizing something. Don't read sentence-case as cold — read it as "I'm being careful with this one".
- **Emoji reactions over text replies** for ack ("👍" or "✅" instead of "ok got it"). Saves everyone a notification.
- **No `@everyone` unless it's actually an incident.** Pings for visibility, not for attention.
- **Memes are fine.** Memes that pile on a person aren't. The line is intent.
