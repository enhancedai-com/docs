## Overview

FluxPrompt connects to Notion through OAuth. Before your users can link their Notion workspaces, you need a **public integration** registered in Notion's developer portal. This guide walks you through creating and configuring that integration.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a Notion integration">
    Go to [notion.so/my-integrations](https://www.notion.so/my-integrations) and click **New integration**.

    - Give it a name (e.g., "FluxPrompt").
    - Select the workspace you want to develop against.
    - Click **Submit**.

    This creates an internal integration by default. You will convert it to a public integration in the next step.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure OAuth">
    In your integration's settings, switch to the **Distribution** tab and enable **Public integration**.

    Set the **Redirect URI** to:

    ```
    https://app.fluxprompt.ai/oauth-callback/integration/notion
    ```

    <Warning>
    The redirect URI must match exactly, including the protocol and path. A mismatch causes OAuth to fail silently or return an `invalid_redirect_uri` error.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set required capabilities">
    Under the **Capabilities** tab, enable the following permissions:

    | Capability | Required |
    |---|---|
    | Read content | Yes |
    | Update content | Yes |
    | Insert content | Yes |

    These are the minimum capabilities needed for both pull and push operations in FluxPrompt flows.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Get your credentials">
    Navigate to the **Secrets** tab (or **OAuth credentials** section) and copy the following values:

    - **OAuth client ID**
    - **OAuth client secret**

    Store these securely. You will add them to your FluxPrompt environment configuration.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Submit for public integration review (production)">
    Before your integration can be used by anyone outside your own workspace, Notion requires a review. Go to the **Distribution** tab and submit the integration for review.

    Notion typically reviews within a few business days. Until approved, only members of the development workspace can authorize the integration.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Summary for your technical team

Add the following to your server environment:

| Variable | Value |
|---|---|
| `NOTION_CLIENT_ID` | OAuth client ID from step 4 |
| `NOTION_CLIENT_SECRET` | OAuth client secret from step 4 |
| Redirect URI | `https://app.fluxprompt.ai/oauth-callback/integration/notion` |

Once these are configured and the integration is approved, your users can connect their Notion workspace directly from the Third Party Integration node.
